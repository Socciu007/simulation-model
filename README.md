# Simulation Model
This model of phytoplankton-zooplankton interaction under hydrodynamic conditions uses a three-dimensional array to simulate the population dynamics of two groups of phytoplankton in a flowing water environment. The model simultaneously integrates phytoplankton growth processes, zooplankton consumption behavior, and the impact of hydrodynamics on their ability to access, capture prey, and spatial distribution. The three-dimensional structure allows for a more detailed examination of nonlinear interactions, patch formation, and complex dynamic phenomena such as cyclic oscillations, population outbreaks, or dynamic phase shifts due to changes in flow velocity.
